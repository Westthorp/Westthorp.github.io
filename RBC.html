3. What has been your greatest non-academic achievement/accomplishment within the last 2 years? (150 words)
The leading and planning of a hiking trip to the lake district, to gain my Mark Evison Foundation Award, was an achievement that I would consider my proudest. The Mark Evison Foundation came to me and I signed up to the initiative with a group of my school peers. Between us we then researched possible routes for a hiking journey and finally decided, in the following January, that an expedition to the Lake District would be within our physical and financial capabilities. The foundation gave us a £500 fund for the trip, which I was then able to use to purchase the relevant meals and equipment for our group in correspondence with our pre-planned budgeting. The team then put me in charge of navigating us across the country to our Hiking spot in Keswick and ensuring the planning and execution of the trip ran smoothly. I contacted the relevant train, coach and taxi companies to organise our transportation to and from our hiking destination. This experience gave me a great sense of responsibility as I needed to ensure that the trip ran smoothly from start to finish not only for myself, but also for my team. This journey separated us from the busy London lifestyle that we had grown so accustomed to and allowed me personally to experience a level of dependability and independence that I will apply to future challenges.
